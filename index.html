About Me

I am originally from Atlanta, Georgia, and recently completed my undergrad at Cornell University with a B.A. in physics. I am now a post-baccalaureate researcher interested in searching for and studying Earth-like exoplanets, and plan to pursue a PhD in astronomy in the next year. Outside of research, I am passionate about public outreach and lowering barriers to entry for early-career researchers.

Research

My work focuses on exoplanet observations with the James Webb Space Telescope. Previously, I have worked to determine whether a potential habitable atmosphere on TRAPPIST-1f would be constrainable despite the activity of its host star, performing atmospheric retrievals on simulated observations. Currently, I am analyzing the infrared spectra of two solitary white dwarf stars for signs of close-orbit planets. Image Credit: W. M. Keck Observatory/Adam Makarenko
